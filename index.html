Fan Race lets you monitor Facebook Like counts in real-time. It works on small screens, but we recommend projecting it against the exterior wall of the building across the street.
It takes up to 3 URL parameters: The we and vs parameters take vanity names or IDs of Facebook pages, e.g. "CocaCola" or "40796308305" for https://www.facebook.com/CocaCola.
we is the page on the left (make it your own or your favourite page!. vs is the (probably evil) page on the right.
The third parameter, party is a YouTube video token (the part after "?v=" in the video URL), e.g. "dQw4w9WgXcQ" for that video. This video's audio will be played celebratorily when your page's Like count surpasses that of the other page. If it doesn't play, it's probably for copyright reasons.
You pass the parameters like so: http://catearcher.github.com/FanRace?we=diesocialisten&vs=facebook&party=7UCm6uyzNE8

There is also a single-player-mode for only one page. To activate it, use the additional URL parameter mode and set it to "single". You can specify your target fan count with the goal parameter. Example:
http://catearcher.github.com/FanRace?we=diesocialisten&mode=single&goal=10000
